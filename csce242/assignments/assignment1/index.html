How to make a Krispy Kreme doughnut at home!

By Emma Roberts

What Makes That "Krispy Kreme" Style?

Homemade Krispy Kreme–style doughnuts can be recreated with a rich, well-hydrated dough, gentle proofing, and careful temperature control. The key is proper rising and frying so they stay light and airy, finished with a simple glaze for a bakery-fresh result.

This guide teaches doughnut-making technique rather than a secret recipe, explaining the reasons behind each step. You’ll mix and proof yeast dough, shape rings, fry evenly at the right temperature, then glaze for a glossy finish. With the provided tips and good organization, you can enjoy warm, melt-in-your-mouth doughnuts at home.

Safety first: Hot oil can cause burns. Use a deep, heavy pot, don’t overfill with oil, and monitor temperature with a thermometer. Keep kids and pets away from the stove while frying.

Key Steps

  1. Mix & Knead: Combine warm milk, a little sugar, and active dry yeast. Add eggs, melted butter, flour, and salt; knead until smooth and slightly tacky.
  2. First Rise: Cover and proof in a warm spot until doubled (about 1 to 1½ hours depending on room temperature).
  3. Shape: Roll to about ½-inch thick. Cut rings with a doughnut cutter or two round cutters; re-roll scraps once.
  4. Second Rise: Place on parchment, lightly cover, and proof until puffy and delicate (about 30–45 minutes).
  5. Fry: Heat neutral oil to 350–360°F (175–182°C). Fry 45–60 seconds per side until light golden; drain on a rack.
  6. Glaze: Dip warm doughnuts into a thin vanilla glaze (powdered sugar + milk + vanilla) and let set 5–10 minutes.

Proofing & Frying Quick Guide

Stage Target What to Look For
First Rise 75–80°F (24–27°C), 60–90 min Dough roughly doubled; bounces back slowly when pressed.
Second Rise 75–80°F (24–27°C), 30–45 min Cut dough feels airy; holds a gentle fingerprint.
Frying 350–360°F (175–182°C) Pale golden color; floats and puffs within seconds.
